# Why I'm starting over (sort of)

*Introducing Castles of the Underground*

---

Since September 2020, I've published 29 releases and 96 tracks under the name GFD.

So why am I launching a new artist name?

## The problem with GFD

GFD started as a name to release my music under. I was writing tracks, releasing them, and seeing what happened.

But five years and nearly 100 tracks later, I've run into some problems:

**The name is generic**: There are artists with similar names. GFD doesn't stand out, and it's easy to confuse with others.

**I'm in danger of flooding Spotify**: 96 tracks in under five years is a lot. There's some good stuff, but some of it hasn't worked so well - particularly the earlier releases. When everything lives under one name, it's hard for listeners to know what to expect.

**The sound has evolved**: GFD started as a mix of everything - dance, funk, instrumentals. Later on, I started adding vocals, adding another style to the mix. I'm starting to find that the dance tracks are connecting with people more than anything else. And I'd like to give those tracks more of a focus.

## The solution: three artists, one home

Instead of trying to put everything under one name, I'm splitting things up:

**Castles of the Underground** - Dance music. This is the new focus. It's a clean slate with room to grow.

**GFD** - Mostly funk. The existing catalogue stays. New funk tracks will still land here, just at a slower pace.

**Roads to Atlantis** - Experimental and videogame-style music. This has always been the weirder stuff, and it'll stay niche, mostly on Bandcamp.

gfdmusic.com becomes the hub for all three. Think of it as a small label rather than a single artist.

## Why "Castles of the Underground"?

GFD was forgettable. I wanted something more distinctive: something that sounds like travelling between places.

Good dance music takes me on a journey. It brings back memories of people and places from long ago. Hidden worlds. Late nights. That feeling of being transported somewhere else entirely.

That's what I want this music to do. Castles of the Underground felt right.

The artwork captures it too: ancient structures dissolving into the cosmos, a lone figure standing between worlds. Somewhere between memory and imagination.

## What's coming

The first Castles of the Underground single is coming very soon.

This Substack is where I'll document the journey. Behind-the-scenes notes, occasional previews, reflections on what's working and what isn't. No fixed schedule, just sharing as it happens.

If you've followed GFD over the years - thank you. That catalogue isn't going anywhere.

If you're just getting on board - welcome to the start of something new.
