# GFD Music - Growth Strategy

Working document for thinking through site growth, content strategy, and artist identity.

---

## Artist Identity (DECIDED)

### The Structure: gfdmusic as a "Label"

**gfdmusic** is the umbrella — the label, not an artist. This solves the social handle problem.

- **gfdmusic.com** = hub site for all projects
- **@gfdmusic** social handles = label account, posts about all artists
- No new handles needed

### Three Artists

| Artist | Genre | Platforms | Status |
|--------|-------|-----------|--------|
| **Castles of the Underground** | Dance (main focus) | Spotify, streaming | LIVE - Jupiter & Sun Rising released |
| **GFD** | Funk | Spotify | Continuing, slower pace (29 releases, legacy) |
| **Roads to Atlantis** | Experimental / videogame | Bandcamp (+ Spotify TBD) | Niche |

### Why This Works

- **CotU gets a clean slate** — no "96 tracks of varying quality" baggage
- **GFD isn't retired** — it's the funk home, continues at slower pace
- **RtA stays weird** — Bandcamp suits the collector/niche audience
- **Solves "flooding"** — three catalogues growing sustainably, not one overloaded artist
- **Dance is the focus** — CotU is where energy goes, since dance is getting traction

### Artwork

CotU artist image is in place. The plan is to repurpose it as a variant for the Terraform album when that's ready — not urgent given the album is a long way off.

### Track Assignment

Track assignment to CotU / GFD / RtA is an ongoing per-track decision, not a one-off sort. A track's direction can shift during production (a remix might become more CotU; a new track could go anywhere). Treat each release decision individually.

### Legacy Cleanup (Optional)

Some older GFD releases may be worth removing from Spotify if they don't represent current quality. Worth flagging candidates, even if not acted on immediately.
